## ‚öñÔ∏è Legal Violations Identified

### 1. IRS Violations (26 U.S.C. ¬ß 501(c)(3))

**Violation:** Substantial lobbying exceeding 501(c)(3) limits

**Evidence:**
- 501(c)(3) organizations may only conduct "insubstantial" lobbying (~5% of activities)
- Coalition has spent $359,053 on lobbying over 5 years (24% of 3-year revenue)
- CEO is registered lobbyist paid $155,000
- Professional lobbying firm paid $107,300
- Professional fees represent 10-13% of annual budget

**Consequence:** Loss of tax-exempt status, back taxes, penalties

### 2. Massachusetts Lobbying Law Violations (M.G.L. c. 3, ¬ß¬ß 39-50)

**Violation:** False expense reporting

**Evidence:**
- Ruth Zakarin reports $0 lobbying expenses (all 6 years)
- Audited financial statements show $96,753 in professional fees
- Charles Group Consulting paid $107,300
- Professional lobbyists cannot operate with zero expenses

**Consequence:** Criminal penalties, fines, debarment

### 3. Campaign Finance Law Violations (M.G.L. c. 55)

**Violation:** Failure to file required ballot committee reports

**Evidence:**
- "Vote Yes for a Safe MA" launched May 12, 2025
- Zero reports filed as of November 23, 2025 (195+ days)
- Penalty: $25/day = $4,875+ owed

**Consequence:** Civil penalties, possible criminal charges

### 4. Grant Fraud

**Violation:** False statements in grant application, misuse of public funds

**Evidence:**
- Grant claimed purpose: "Public health education"
- Reality: Organization's primary function is lobbying
- Grant receipt caused 70% spike in professional fees
- Staff hired May 1, campaign launched May 12

**Consequence:** Criminal fraud charges, repayment, debarment
